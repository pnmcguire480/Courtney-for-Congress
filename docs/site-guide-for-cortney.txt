4. HOW TO ADD A BLOG POST
---------------------------------------------------------

You have two options:

OPTION A — SEND IT TO ME (easiest)
  1. Write your post in any format — email, text message,
     Google Doc, voice memo, handwritten note, whatever.
  2. Include:
       - A title (short and punchy)
       - The content (a few paragraphs in your own voice)
       - What category it is: Campaign Update, Statement,
         Event Recap, Press Release, or Endorsement
  3. Send it to pnmcguire4@gmail.com or text 813-940-0111
  4. I will format it, add it to the site, and let you know
     when it is live. Usually within 24 hours.

OPTION B — DO IT YOURSELF (if you are feeling adventurous)
  If you have access to the GitHub repository and want to
  add a post directly:

  1. Go to the news/ folder in the project files
  2. Find the file called _template.html — this is your
     blank blog post template
  3. Make a copy of it
  4. Rename the copy using this format:
       YYYY-MM-DD-short-description.html
     For example:
       2026-03-25-signature-milestone.html
       2026-04-01-town-hall-recap.html
  5. Open the file and replace these placeholders:
       - "POST TITLE" with your actual title
       - "POST DESCRIPTION" with a 1-2 sentence summary
       - "YYYY-MM-DD" with the date (e.g., 2026-03-25)
       - "Month DD, YYYY" with the readable date
         (e.g., March 25, 2026)
       - "X min read" with an estimate (count your
         paragraphs — 3-4 paragraphs is about 2 min)
       - "Campaign Update" with the post category
       - The template body text with your actual content
  6. Use simple HTML for your content:
       <p>Your paragraph here.</p>
       <h2>A Section Heading</h2>
       <ul><li>A bullet point</li></ul>
       <blockquote>"A strong quote."</blockquote>
  7. Save the file, commit it, and push to GitHub. Netlify
     will auto-deploy it within about 30 seconds.

  NOTE: You also need to add a link to the new post on the
  news listing page (news/index.html). If that part feels
  complicated, just do steps 1-6 and I will handle the rest.

Honestly? Option A is the move. Just send me your words and
I will make it look great. Your time is better spent knocking
doors and collecting signatures.

7. PHOTO GUIDELINES
---------------------------------------------------------

Photos are the single biggest thing your site needs right now.
The design is ready — it just needs YOUR face and YOUR story
in it.

WHAT MAKES A GOOD CAMPAIGN PHOTO:

  DO:
  - Show you talking to real people (not posing)
  - Capture candid moments — laughing, listening, gesturing
  - Include Ohio settings people recognize — neighborhoods,
    coffee shops, community centers, downtown Cleveland
  - Shoot in natural light when possible (near windows,
    outdoors)
  - Get a mix of close-ups (face and shoulders) and wider
    shots (full body with environment)
  - Include volunteers, supporters, and community members

  DO NOT:
  - No stiff posed photos where everyone is standing in a
    line staring at the camera (group photos are fine if
    people look natural)
  - No American-flag-draped politician poses — your visual
    identity is "Ohio working class," not "generic candidate"
  - No stock photo energy — documentary, not corporate
  - No AI-generated images

FILE FORMAT AND SIZE:
  - Phone photos are fine. Just make sure they are not blurry.
  - Shoot at the highest quality your phone allows.
  - Landscape orientation (horizontal) is more versatile for
    the website, but portrait (vertical) works too.
  - Send me the original files — do not resize or compress
    them. I will optimize for the web.
  - If you can, take photos with your phone's regular camera
    (not Instagram or Snapchat, which compress the image).

THE MUST-HAVE SHOTS (in priority order):
  1. Professional headshot — for press, yard signs, everywhere
  2. Full-body environmental portrait — for the homepage hero
  3. Family photo — with your sons, authentic not posed
  4. At-work / nursing photo — your credibility on healthcare
  5. Speaking at an event — authority, passion
  6. Talking one-on-one with a voter — connection
  7. Canvassing / door knocking — grassroots proof
  8. Volunteer team photo — campaign crew
  9. Ohio neighborhood / landscape shots — atmosphere

PRO TIP FOR EVENTS:
Assign one person at every event to be the "photo person."
Their only job for 10 minutes is to walk around and snap
20-30 photos. You will use maybe 5 of them, but you need
the volume to get the good ones.

We have a more detailed photo shot list on file. If you
are booking a photographer or want to share specific shot
ideas with your team, let me know and I will send it over.

8. BLOG POST IDEAS
---------------------------------------------------------

You do not need to write long essays. A few paragraphs in
your own voice is all it takes. Here are topics ready to go:

1. "WHY I AM RUNNING AS AN INDEPENDENT"
   Your philosophy on breaking free from the two-party
   system. Why you chose this harder path. What it means
   for the voters of OH-11.

2. "SIGNATURE DRIVE UPDATE — WHERE WE STAND"
   A weekly or biweekly update on signature collection.
   How many you have, stories from the trail, where people
   can find you to sign. Great for building urgency as
   May 4th approaches.

3. "WHAT I LEARNED KNOCKING DOORS THIS WEEK"
   Pick the most interesting conversation you had while
   canvassing. What did voters tell you? What surprised you?
   What broke your heart? What gave you hope?

4. "FOLLOW THE MONEY — HOW YOUR OPPONENT IS FUNDED"
   Walk people through the FEC data on your own terms. Who
   is writing checks to your opponent? What do those donors
   want? How does that compare to your grassroots funding?

5. "A NURSE'S CASE FOR MEDICARE FOR ALL"
   You have 12 years of firsthand experience watching the
   healthcare system fail patients. Tell that story. No
   policy paper — just what you have seen with your own eyes.

6. "[EVENT NAME] RECAP — WHAT HAPPENED AND WHAT IS NEXT"
   After any major event, write a quick recap. What happened,
   who showed up, what you heard, and what is coming next.
   Include photos.

7. "MY SONS ARE THE REASON I AM IN THIS FIGHT"
   Your story as a mom, raising a teenager and a young son
   with autism. What kind of country do you want them to
   grow up in? What keeps you up at night?

8. "THE GOLDEN RULE IN POLITICS"
   Your guiding principle — treating people the way you want
   to be treated — and how that applies to policymaking.
   What would Congress look like if every member lived by
   this rule?

9. "WHAT 'UNBOUGHT' ACTUALLY MEANS"
   Explain what it is like to run a campaign with zero
   corporate money. What you have to do differently. Why
   it is harder — and why it is worth it.

10. "ENDORSEMENT SPOTLIGHT: [NAME]"
    When you get an endorsement, write a short post about
    who they are, why their support matters, and what it
    says about the coalition you are building.

11. "HOUSING IN OH-11 — THE NUMBERS ARE BRUTAL"
    Pick one issue from your platform and make it local.
    What does the housing crisis look like in your specific
    neighborhoods? Use real numbers, real stories.

12. "I TESTIFIED AT THE OHIO STATEHOUSE — HERE IS WHAT I SAID"
    You spoke against Senate Bill 87. Write about what that
    experience was like. What you said. How it felt. What
    the legislators' reactions were.

13. "HOW TO HELP RIGHT NOW (EVEN IF YOU HAVE 10 MINUTES)"
    A practical post listing 5-10 things supporters can do
    right now — share a post, sign the petition, bring a
    friend, donate $5, put up a yard sign.

14. "WHAT 'PEOPLE-POWERED' LOOKS LIKE IN REAL LIFE"
    Show people the behind-the-scenes of a grassroots
    campaign. The kitchen-table strategy sessions. The
    hand-addressed envelopes. The volunteer who drove 45
    minutes to help canvass.

15. "FROM POLITICAL COMA TO RUNNING FOR CONGRESS"
    The full version of your awakening story — the brain
    procedure, the moment of clarity, the decision to run.
    This is your most powerful narrative. Tell it fully.
