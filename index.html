Who She Is

Meet Cortney

A nurse and mom fighting for working families in Ohio's 11th district. Cortney grew up downtown Ashtabula, Ohio in a hardworking family. She's spent the last 12 years working as a licensed practical nurse — caring for patients, fighting for the vulnerable, and learning firsthand what it looks like when a system fails the people it's supposed to serve.

A rare condition required a stent to be placed in her brain to restore normal pressure and blood flow. After her procedure in October 2025, she's feeling better than she has in years — and that radical change in her life fueled her passion to help people on a larger scale.

"I spent most of my life in a political coma. Then, a life-changing procedure restored blood flow to my brain, and I awoke. Suddenly, I became fully aware of the corruption and inept leadership representing the American people. I realized that members of Congress were casting votes not on behalf of their constituents, but for the lobbyists who lined their pockets. In that moment, I knew that if I want my sons to inherit a sustainable future, I will have to fight for it myself."

Cleveland's 11th District is majority-Black, deeply working class, and built on generations of resilience. Cortney has spent her nursing career in this community — caring for the families, the elders, the children who deserve representation that actually fights for them. She once believed nursing was her true calling. But as national crises deepen, she's come to realize her lifelong defense of the "little guy" has been preparing her for public service all along.

My Guiding Principle: The Golden Rule

Living by the golden rule, I treat others with the same respect I seek, uphold others' dignity, live my life with integrity, speak out against injustice, and protect those who cannot defend themselves.

"I am NOT a politician."

Instead, I want to be a POLICYMAKER and someone who fights for fairness and equity for the working class. I have had seasons of feast and seasons of famine. I have two wonderful sons — one very bright teenager and one very bright little guy who receives therapies for his autism spectrum disorder. I am also a proud pet parent to 4 rescues — 2 dogs, 2 cats. I am joining the fight to eliminate corruption in DC and to end America's complicity in genocide.

The Question Everyone Asks

Why Independent?

Because the two-party system has failed working people. The incumbent in Ohio's 11th takes 59% of her campaign funding from PACs — corporate money, foreign-interest money, Super PAC money. Party loyalty is how they keep you from demanding better.

I'm not a spoiler. I'm the alternative. When both parties answer to the same donors, voting for either one isn't a choice — it's an illusion. An independent candidate funded entirely by working people is the only vote that actually threatens the machine.

Cleveland deserves a representative who answers to Cleveland — not to AIPAC, not to Big Pharma, not to the party bosses who decide which candidates get funding and which get crushed.

Every dollar in this campaign comes from real people. When I get to Congress, I answer to you — not to the groups that wrote the checks. That's the difference.
